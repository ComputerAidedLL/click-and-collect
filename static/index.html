Syntax rules

Sequent syntax

A, B, ..., Zformulas
,to separate formulas
( and )around a sub-formula
|- or ⊢thesis sign

Example:A0,A1,A2,B |- A',A'',Γ

Linear logic formulas

^dual (e.g. A^)
* or ⊗tensor (multiplicative conjunction)
| or ⅋par (multiplicative disjunction)
&with (additive conjunction)
+ or ⊕plus (additive disjunction)
1one (multiplicative conjunction unit)
bot or ⊥ or _bottom (multiplicative disjunction unit)
top or ⊤ or Ttop (additive conjunction unit)
0zero (additive disjunction unit)
-o or ⊸lollipop (linear implication)
!"of course"
?"why not"

Example:!(A&B) |- !A*!B

Tutorial

Click on a formula or its orthogonal to apply axiom rule. |-A,A^

Alternatively, you can click on thesis symbol. |-1,1^

Click on a connector to apply rule. |-A|A^

Move formula within the list before applying tensor rule. Formulas before tensor will be the context on left hand-side, and formulas after tensor will be the context on right hand-side. |-A,A^,A*A^

As for plus rule, just click on the left or right formula. |-A,B+A^

For whynot, you have three possibilities. Single click on whynot connector applies weakening, single click on formula applies dereliction, and double click on either connector or formula applies contraction. |-?A,?B,?(A^*A^)

That's it!
