Syntax rules

Sequent syntax

A, B, ..., Zformulas
,to separate formulas
( and )around a sub-formula
|- or ⊢thesis sign

Example: A0,A1,A2,B |- A',A'',Γ

Linear logic formulas

^dual (e.g. A^)
* or ⊗tensor (multiplicative conjunction)
| or ⅋par (multiplicative disjunction)
&with (additive conjunction)
+ or ⊕plus (additive disjunction)
1one (multiplicative conjunction unit)
bot or ⊥ or _bottom (multiplicative disjunction unit)
top or ⊤ or Ttop (additive conjunction unit)
0zero (additive disjunction unit)
-o or ⊸lollipop (linear implication)
!"of course"
?"why not"

Example: !(A&B) |- !A*!B

Tutorial

Click on a formula or its orthogonal to apply axiom rule.

|-A,A^

If the two formulas are not explicitly orthogonal, you can click on thesis symbol to attempt to apply axiom rule on a two-formulas sequent.

|-1,1^

Click on connectors to apply related rule.

|-A|A^

Move formulas within the list before applying tensor rule. Formulas before tensor will be in the context on left hand-side, and formulas after tensor will be in the context on right hand-side.

|-A,A^,A*A^

As for plus rule, just click on the left or right formula.

|-A,B+A^

For whynot connector, you have three possibilities:

  • single click on whynot connector applies weakening
  • single click on formula applies dereliction
  • double click on either connector or formula applies contraction.
|-?A,?B,A^*A^

That's it! You should now be able to have all proofs on this tutorial complete (with green background). Have fun!
